01 / Manufacturing
How a TBM is Built
A TBM is a custom-engineered product — no two are identical. Each machine is designed specifically for the geological conditions of its project. The process from contract to site delivery takes 12–24 months.
Manufacturing Time12–24 months
Typical Weight2,000 – 4,000 t
Diameter Range3m – 17.6m
Main ManufacturersHerrenknecht, Robbins, CREG
Eurasia Tunnel TBM ⌀13.66 m
⚙
Design & Engineering
Custom cutterhead geometry, EPB vs. slurry TBM decision based on soil reports. FEM analysis.
🔩
Component Fabrication
Main bearing, shield, cutterhead arms produced in steel mills. Tolerances: ±0.1mm on critical joints.
🏭
Factory Assembly & Testing
Full trial assembly at manufacturer's plant. Hydraulic, electrical, PLC systems tested.
🚢
Disassembly & Shipping
Machine is cut into transportable sections (max ~80t per piece). Shipped by sea or heavy-load truck.
⬇
Site Reassembly in Launch Shaft
Lowered section by section using cranes. Reassembled underground in the launch chamber.
02 / Workforce
Who Works on a TBM?
TBM operations run 24/7 in rotating shifts. A typical metro project employs 60–120 people directly on the TBM drive, plus hundreds more in support roles above ground.
TBM OPERATOR
2–4
per shift. Controls advance speed, face pressure, grout injection. 5+ years training.
SEGMENT ERECTOR
3–5
per shift. Installs 6 precast segments per ring using robotic erector arm.
MAINTENANCE CREW
6–10
Cutter inspection, hydraulic maintenance. Intervention in compressed air (up to 3.5 bar).
SURVEYING TEAM
2–3
Laser guidance, total station. Keeps TBM on alignment within ±20mm.
LOGISTICS CREW
8–15
Segment transport, muck train operation, supply chain coordination.
ENGINEERS & PM
10–20
Geotechnical, structural, electrical, health & safety, project management.
Key insight: TBM operators are a globally scarce resource — the same crews travel from project to project worldwide. The Doha Metro imported specialists from Europe, Japan, and Australia.
03 / Site Setup
How a TBM Site Works
A TBM construction site is unlike a conventional building site. It is a linear underground factory with a fixed surface footprint but unlimited depth.
📍
Site Establishment
Launch shaft excavated (typically 20–40m deep, 20×30m plan). Retaining walls, dewatering systems installed.
🏗
Segment Casting Plant
Precast factory set up on site or within 30km. Produces ~20–30 rings/day. Each ring = 6 segments + 1 key segment.
🚇
TBM Launch
Machine pushed against reaction frame, boring begins. First 50m is the "learning curve" — slow and cautious.
🚂
Continuous Operation
Rail-mounted muck trains carry spoil out. Segment trains bring rings in. Two trains pass on parallel tracks.
🏁
Breakthrough
TBM drives into reception shaft. Celebrated publicly — breakthrough ceremonies are major PR events in Istanbul.
Surface Footprint~2,000–5,000 m²
Avg Advance Rate8–20 m/day
Marmaray Daily Rate~10 m/day

Environmental Impact
Compared to cut-and-cover construction, TBM tunnelling is significantly less disruptive — but not impact-free.
Surface DisruptionLOW (vs cut-and-cover)
Noise at SurfaceLOW–MODERATE
Groundwater ImpactMODERATE (dewatering)
Carbon (Concrete/Steel)HIGH
Spoil Volume per km~50,000–100,000 m³
Circular Economy: Grand Paris Express recycles 70% of spoil as construction aggregate. Istanbul Gayrettepe–Airport line spoil was used for coastal fill projects.

Vibration monitoring: Sensitive buildings above the tunnel require continuous monitoring. Settlements must stay below 15–25mm typically.
